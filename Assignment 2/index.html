2020 Global Top 2000 Companies

Forbes has published its Global 2000 ranking, which showcases the worlds 2,000 public companies as of May 13 2020. These corporate giants collectively represent a $42.3 trillion, in revenues. Contribute $3.3 trillion in profits. Furthermore they command assets $201.4 trillion. Possess a combined market capitalization of $54.3 trillion. It is important to note that although sales and assets have experienced growth compared to the year there has been a decline in profits and market capitalization.

The unexpected turbulence in markets due to the COVID 19 pandemic has significantly impacted the market values of companies listed in the Global 2000 ranking. The market values presented in this ranking were determined as of April 30th reflecting the shifts within the landscape. This years threshold for entry into this group of companies was set at a market value of $5.27 billion, which is notably lower than the preceding years threshold of $6.25 billion.

This interactive dashboard provides an exploration of the Global 2000 companies offering a view of their geographic presence, regional distribution and financial performance. By utilizing this tool you can gain insights, into the corporate landscape.

Location

The map visualization gives a picture of where the Top 2000 companiesre located around the world. Each marker, on the map represents a continent. Is color coded accordingly. The size of the markers indicates the market value, which is calculated by dividing the market value by the number of Top 2000 companies in that particular location.

Interestingly among all countries The Netherlands stands out with an market value of $32.23 billion. This comes as no surprise since The Netherlands has a total market value and a smaller number of Top 2000 companies compared to other nations. This combination contributes to its average market value.

On the hand Greece has the average market value among all countries with just $2.24 billion. Although Greece does have ten Top 2000 companies its overall low total market value puts it at the bottom of the list.

In contrast countries, like China, the United Kingdom, Japan and the United States have total market values but also more Top 2000 companies. This slightly reduces their market values compared to The Netherlands. However these countries still have a higher average market values compared to other nations.

Lastly, while there are only two countries in Oceania that house Top 2000 companies, Australia's ability to achieve a notably high mean market value is indeed an impressive accomplishment, which emphasizing its significant economic position, on a global scale.
